Hi there! I'm Scott Stanchfield. Welcome to 605.486, the online offering of Mobile Application Development for the Android Platform! You can view my biography under Syllabus & Course Information on the left navigation menu. Let me give you an overview of what we will be doing this semester and where you should begin.

We'll be exploring Android application development, covering all sorts of topics including creation of user interfaces, storing data, and communicating with other devices and servers. You can see the full list of topics under the Course Outline section of the Syllabus & Course Information area. Be sure to pay close attention to the Course Outline! It'll tell you which modules are covered each week as well as start and due dates for the assignments.

Note that the module layout for this course is a little different than most of the online courses you may have taken. Rather than each week being a single module, most weeks contain multiple modules. I decided to break the course into modules by topic rather than by week, in an attempt to keep my sanity. I'm not sure it worked, as development of 31 (including Module 0) course modules feels like more work than development of 14…

In case you want to work offline, I've made the entire content (videos, slides, and sample code) available for download in five zip files. You can download these via the Downloadable Resources link on the left navigation menu. You can also download all the assignment descriptions at the start of the class via the Assignments link.

If you have questions during the course, please:

  • FIRST: search using Google or StackOverflow for ideas. You can often find a response faster than I will see your question and respond
  • SECOND: check the forum and see if the question has been asked
  • THIRD: If the question isn't private (such as a question about your grades), please post it in the discussion forums)
  • FOURTH: If it's a private matter, please email me at scott@javadude.com

As you progress through the course, if you have comments about the content (things you really like, dislike, or how things can be done better), please post in the Course Feedback forum or keep a log of your thoughts to send to me after the course is finished.

If you have any questions or concerns while going through this course you may post them in the General Questions thread, located under Discussions on the left menu or you may contact me directly at scott@javadude.com. Additionally, you can refer to Help & Support on the left menu for a listing of all the student services and support available to you.
